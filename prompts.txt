## SCORING RUBRIC

Score each dimension on a 1-10 scale (integers only). Then compute the overall score using this exact formula:

score = (hard_skills_match × 0.35) + (experience_level_alignment × 0.20) + (project_impact_alignment × 0.20) + (responsibility_complexity_match × 0.10) + (education_certification × 0.05) + (keywords_ats_compatibility × 0.10)

Round the final score to the nearest integer. Place the result in the "score" field. Do NOT include the arithmetic in any output field.

Round the final score to one decimal place.

| Dimension                          | Weight | Criteria |
|------------------------------------|--------|----------|
| Hard Skills Match                  | 35%    | What percentage of required and preferred skills (tools, technologies, methodologies, platforms, languages) from the JD are explicitly demonstrated in the resume with real project/work usage? Penalize missing "required" skills heavily. Give partial credit for closely related skills only if clearly transferable. |
| Experience Level Alignment         | 20%    | Does the candidate's total years of relevant experience, seniority progression, and scope of past roles (team size led, ownership level, decision-making authority) match what the JD demands? A JD asking for Senior with 5+ YOE should score low for a candidate with 1 YOE regardless of skill match. |
| Project & Impact Alignment         | 20%    | Do the resume's projects, achievements, and responsibilities mirror the kind of work described in the JD? Prioritize quantified impact — metrics, scale, business outcomes (e.g., "reduced costs by 30%", "grew user base by 200%", "managed $2M budget"). Penalize vague bullet points with no measurable results. |
| Responsibility & Complexity Match  | 10%    | Does the resume demonstrate handling the same level of responsibility, scope, and problem complexity that the JD implies? Consider: cross-functional collaboration, stakeholder management, independent ownership, scale of operations, strategic vs tactical work. A JD expecting someone to lead initiatives should score low for a resume showing only task execution. |
| Education & Certifications         | 5%     | Does the candidate meet the stated educational requirements? Are relevant certifications present? Only penalize heavily if the JD explicitly mandates a specific degree or credential with no alternative stated. |
| Keywords & ATS Compatibility       | 10%    | How well does the resume use the exact terminology, acronyms, tool names, and phrases from the JD that an Applicant Tracking System would scan for? Check for exact matches — e.g., JD says "Agile/Scrum" but resume only says "iterative development" = partial match, not full. |

### Score Interpretation Guide (follow this strictly):
- **9-10**: Near-perfect match. Candidate checks almost every required and preferred box. Ready for interview.
- **7-8**: Strong match. All critical requirements met, only minor or "nice-to-have" gaps exist.
- **5-6**: Moderate match. Several important skills or experience-level gaps. Candidate could be a stretch hire.
- **3-4**: Weak match. Major misalignment in required skills, experience level, or scope of work.
- **1-2**: Poor match. Resume is for a fundamentally different role, field, or career level.

1. **IDENTIFY** what is missing or weak in the resume relative to the JD.
2. **LOCATE** exactly where in the resume the fix should go (e.g., "in your summary/objective section", "under your XYZ Company role", "in the skills section", "add a new bullet under project ABC").
3. **PROVIDE an example rewrite** using the JD's exact language and terminology so it passes ATS filters.
4. **PRIORITIZE** improvements by impact — list the highest-impact change first (the one most likely to get the resume past the screener).
5. **Do not limit the improvements** — Suggest all improvements that can be made to the resume. At the same time don't just focus on quantity but quality



### Improvement format example:
Each improvement should be in a numeric bullet each separated by a new line. Use the below example
"1. [MISSING: Stakeholder Management] → Under your Role at ABC Corp, add a bullet: 'Led cross-functional alignment between engineering, product, and design teams, presenting weekly progress updates to VP-level stakeholders.' This directly addresses the JD's requirement for 'strong stakeholder communication skills.'"

### CRITICAL: No Fabricated Metrics
When suggesting new bullet points or rewrites, NEVER invent specific percentages or numbers
(e.g., "reduced latency by 15%"). Instead:
- Use placeholders: "reduced API response time by [X]%" for the candidate to fill in
- You may reference metrics already present in the resume and reframe them, but do not generate new ones
Fabricated metrics damage candidate credibility in interviews.


### Authenticity Rule
Only suggest additions that the candidate could plausibly defend in an interview based on their existing roles and projects. If a suggestion requires experience the candidate may not actually have, prefix it with [VERIFY] and add a note: "Only include this if you have genuine experience with [X]." Never instruct the candidate to claim skills or accomplishments they haven't demonstrated.
